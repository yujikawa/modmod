## Why

AI agents (like LLMs) often struggle with the correct YAML structure and visual layout of Modscape models. Specifically:
1.  **Schema Misunderstandings**: AI agents sometimes place layout information (x, y coordinates) inside the `tables` section instead of the dedicated root-level `layout` section.
2.  **Messy Visuals**: AI-generated coordinates are often random or misaligned, leading to overlapping nodes and difficult-to-read diagrams.
3.  **Ambiguous Terminology**: Some descriptions in the current `rules.md` allow for multiple interpretations, leading to inconsistent modeling.

Fixing this will ensure that AI-generated models are both technically valid and visually professional.

## What Changes

- **Strict YAML Schema Definitions**: Explicitly define where each section (especially `layout`) belongs.
- **Prohibition Rules**: Explicitly forbid placing coordinates within `tables` or `domains`.
- **"Beautiful Layout" Heuristics**: Introduce numeric standards for grid alignment (40px increments), standard sizing, and directional flows (Lineage: Left-to-Right, ER: Top-to-Bottom).
- **Domain Layout Clarity**: Define how child tables should be positioned relative to their domain container.
- **Refined Storytelling Rules**: Demand higher quality and more diverse `sampleData`.

## Impact

- **Templates**: `src/templates/rules.md` will be the primary file updated.
- **AI Behavior**: Improved consistency and "cleanliness" of models generated by AI agents.
- **System Stability**: Reduced risk of YAML parsing errors due to schema violations.
